Flight Dynamics: Vertical Movement
Take Off, Landing, Hovering
Each spinning propeller creates a lift. If the sum of the lift created by the four propellers are greater than the weight of the drones, the drone will take off. On the other hand, if the lift is less than the weight of the drone, the drone will fly down. Lastly, if the lift is equal to the weight of the drone, the drone will stay still vertically.
For information about lift, check the previous chapter!
Calculation Example:
If each propeller creates a 2N lift and the weight of the drone is 7N, how will the drone move vertically ?
Yes, the drone will fly up! This is because the lift created by the four propellers is equal to 2N x 4 = 8N, which is greater the weight of the drone.

Flight Dynamics: Lateral Movement - Left/Right
Left/Right
Increasing the rotation speed of a propeller pair on a particular side of the drone will generate more lift than the opposite side pair. This will cause the drone to pitch, and move towards the direction of the side where there is less lift. For the drone to move to the left, the lifts from propellers 2,4 must be greater than that of propellers 1,3.For the drone to move to the right, the sum lift from propellers 1,3 must be greater than that of propellers 2,4.
Creating an unequal amount of lift between different propeller pair is the key to make any lateral movement.
Calculation Example:
Suppose propeller 1,3 each creates a 1.2 N lifts, and propeller 2,4 each creates a 0.8N lift. What would happen?
The sum lifts of propeller 1,3 is greater than the sum lift created by propellers 2,4 (2.4N >1.6N), so the drone move to the right.
